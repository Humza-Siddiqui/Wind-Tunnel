Introduction

Main objective of this lab was to see how the Cl and Cd varies with addition of different types of winglets to the Eppler 212 finite wing. Firstly, to account for gravity bias we run the wind tunnel at 10 Hz and record the normal and axial forces going from-15째 to 25째. Then we insert Eppler 212 without winglets and record the axial and normal forces at-15째 to 25째. This process is repeated for Re = 100 k and 200 K. Then the winglet is added to Eppler 212 and the process is repeated again. Winglets belong to 3 families. 1- Different positions. 2 Different angles. 3- Different lengths. In total 3 winglets are tested from each family. Drag polar graph was plotted for each winglet. From the graph we can see that the best winglet was 60 deg since it had higher Cl value at Re 100 K and 200 K. Also 60 deg winglets had lower Cd values for both 100 K and 200 K Re. We could also see that Cl increases with an increase in Re. And Cd decreases with an increase Re. In conclusion Re has a positive relation with Cl and a negative relation with Cd. So from this experiment we can conclude that higher angle, short length and mid position gives us the best results from all 3 families.

From figure 3 we can conclude that put of all 3 positions mid position gives us the best results. But the result of no winglet is still better. From figure 4 we can conclude that no winglet performed the best when compared to the others. But if we have to use winglets we can use short size since it has the least Cd. None of the lengths contributed to increasing the Cl of Eppler 212.

Figure 5 (Drag polar for different angle winglets)

From figure 5 we can conclude that the 60 degree winglet is the most effective winglet angle from this family. Since it has the highest Cl and the lowest Cd for both the Re values.

Conclusion

We can conclude that the most optimal winglet would have a short length of about 1.84 inches and be positioned in the middle of the wing. This winglet should have an angle of 60 degrees. This winglet would have low induced drag due to the wing tips altering the shape of the vortices behind the wing. Also the friction drag would be low since the wing is short and would have less area exposed to the incoming flow which does increase the skin friction drag but not by a lot. Lastly, the wing will have a higher stall angle so it can maneuver better in the flow. This wing would make it so the fuel consumption is low hence more economical.
